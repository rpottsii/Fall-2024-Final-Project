Why are There so Many Vacant Residential Properties in the City of Syracuse?

Property condition and population decrease are among the main causes.

By Rickie Potts

Most of Syracuse's vacant residential properties are in the south central and northern areas of the city. Map by Datawrapper

If you were to drive around a more than 200-year-old city for long enough, you are almost certain to see a little bit of everything. From historical landmarks to older architecture and brand new developments, Syracuse, New York has no shortage of any of these. Another commonality that can be seen thorughout the city is vacant housing.

Since October of 2022, more than 1,300 residential properties have been filed for vacancy. The question on everyone's mind is, when and why did all of this happen? The answer may be simpler or more complex than you may think.

A cause of Syracuse’s vacant property issue is economic decline. As a rust belt city, Syracuse's economy transformed as factories closed and moved overseas, which in turn left thousands of residents unemployed and therefore, unable to afford housing.

Also, a decline in population can be attributed to the cities large number of vacant residences, as it has dropped from over 200,000 to less than 150,000 people. As a result, many people left the city and this led to many properties being abandoned.

Another cause that can be related to the amount of residential vacancies in Syracuse is the overall condition of many properties in the city. In recent years, many families have filed complaints for lead paint poisoning, something that has affected people of all ages.

In addition, many of the homes in the city were built in the mid 20th century. Over time, they have naturally decayed and many owners do not have the financial means to maintain these properties, which has contributed to the vacancy rate.

There are many legal issues that have led to the high number of vacacies as well. Properties that are in foreclosure processes or disputes have been known to be vacant for years.

Ofiicials and companies face challenges in identifying and transferring ownership of these properties. This leaves many homes without active ownership for months or years at a time.

Another collateral cause of abandonment of homes is decrease in property value. Rundown homes are not visually appealing to the majority of people, which leaves the city and many residences in it without incentive for people to move to Syracuse.

Median selling prices for houses in Syracuse increased by 9% in early 2023. Chart by Rocket Homes

While vacant properties are a significant concern, Syracuse’s housing market has shown signs of resilience in recent years. According to housing data, median home prices in Syracuse increased by 9% in early 2023, signaling renewed interest in the city’s real estate. This upward trend suggests that there is potential for revitalization, particularly if vacant properties can be rehabilitated and integrated into the housing market.
